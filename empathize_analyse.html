'A day in a life of' method to enhance empathy between managers and clients

My professional thesis concerns the 'A day in a life of' method and its use to enable empathy and understanding between two persons. This method could be very useful to enhance the customer orientation and so on the client's experience. Therefore, choose to set up this method with top managers is motivated by some factors : there are decision makers in terms of strategy and objectives, and they develop a corporate culture.
But, in most cases, the method is more like one of famous tv shows: without the other, with intermediate and where the initial stereotype minimize the empathy and understanding. So with my researchs, I identified some principles on which I rebuild the method.
Because of some time constraints, I couldn't apply my finding on top managers but I choose to apply it on my family and friends on a subject with a lot of stereotypes : the gaming (discover some pictures on the right).
The results show that the rebuilt method worked very well with a deep change in social representations in both groups, novices and experts.

Set up new managerial practices to enhance customer orientation in teams

SGSS is a subsidiary of Société Générale and they commissioned our service at BVA to evaluate their client culture. Indeed, they have done a internal project on this dimension. With the tool COS®, which assess the culture client on dimensions organizational and individual and interviews, we evaluated the level of customer orientation on both dimensions. Even if their project laid the foundation of a strong customer orientation, the collaborators were waiting for strong actions.
We organized various groups with top managers to identified some actions to set up and in particular new managerial rituals.

Enhance the quality of work life of a service by analyze each work dimensions

During my mission in hospital public service, one manager requested an action to enhance a stressful situation lived by a secretariat service. These collaborators were very tense for some times because of various factors.
Although their profound involvment, the backlog became too difficult to handle and they didn't know how to solve the problem.
So, in this context, I proposed to receive the team during a group and understand their problematic. To understand the all dimensions of their work problematic, I did a group interview in which I used the DARES grid to review the 6 work dimensions.
This method enabled the professional group to identify concrete solutions to set up with their manager and the other services with whom they worked.

Evaluate the psychosocial risks

In order to revise the Professional Risk Evaluation Document, I created an assessment questionnaire which asked questions on each dimensions of the psychosocial risks. This asessment contained on part qualitative with semi-open questions, an adaptation of the TMS tool created by INRS and the WOCCQ - SPPN questionnaire (Colletive diagnostic method of the psychosocial risks in work).
One part of the tool was used in one-to-one interview, which I realised, and one part administrated on line. I completed the evaluation with the analysis of the health and security indicators.
All the results were aggregated by profession unities.
